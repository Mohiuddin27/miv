ABOUT MIV The Worldwide Amazon Stores Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Team introduces Making the Invisible Visible. A global initiative developed to intentionally and thoughtfully shine a light on the contributions of Amazonians that may go unnoticed or underappreciated. Observance months will give us the opportunity to celebrate trailblazers in different communities including Black, Women, Asian and Pacific Islander, LGBTQIA+, Latinx, Persons with Disabilities, Indigenous, and Veterans.

Introducing the Making the Invisible Visible Trailblazer Award!

The MIV Trailblazer Award, acknowledges exceptional Amazonians who go the extra mile in fostering an inclusive and equitable workplace & community.

As we conclude the celebration of Asian Pacific American Heritage Month, we are excited to announce the opening of nominations for the Pride Trailblazer Awards!

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I ensure my examples demonstrate a high bar?

Examples should be discussed in the STAR format. Additionally, examples should show that the nominee was/is a major contributor to building equity for the LGBTQIA+ community. Use data that quantifies the impact of the project, initiative, program, etc. For example: Surveying the growth and development of her city and recognizing a gap in after-school activities for students in underserved communities, Janet led the efforts between Amazon and AfterSchool2Day to build a mentorship program for LGBTQIA+ high school students living below the poverty line. As a result of this partnership, AfterSchool2Day launched the AfterSchool Academy supported by Janet’s Amazon team and her Glamazon chapter. The Academy's purpose is to empower LGBTQIA+ students in underfunded communities to engage in engaging, safe, and fun after school activities. Since Janet helped program manage AfterSchool2Day's initiatives in San Francisco, over 850 students have been able to attend the Academy. AfterSchool2Day and Amazon continue to have a strong relationship and are looking to build more programs for underfunded communities in the near future.

Can I nominate myself?

Yes. You may nominate someone else or yourself, as long as you tell your story and share your impact!

Can I nominate anonymously?

No. Nominations cannot be done anonymously. Our judging panel may have follow up questions or clarifications and we need to be able to find you!

Will multiple nominations improve my chances at selection?

No. Multiple nominations will not improve your chances. Duplicate nominations will be considered as a single entry.

Can I nominate someone who doesn’t work at Amazon or has worked at Amazon in the past, but is no longer an employee?

No. Although many of us are fortunate to know trailblazers all around, the Making the Invisible, Visible campaign is for current, full-time Amazonians.

Will winners need to provide additional information?

Yes. If you are one of the winners, you will need to provide a headshot. The WW Amazon Stores DEI team will provide more information at this stage.
